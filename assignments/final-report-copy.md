# Final Report Copy

### Assignment: Contribution Process Report

#### Objective:

The goal of this assignment is to provide a detailed summary of the contribution process to an Open Source Software (OSS) project. You will report your experience from deciding on the project until the last activity. This report should include the pros, cons, lessons learned, and how each step helped you or your group. The report can be diary-based, mentioning how things evolved weekly, or activity-based, with each section focusing on one step of the process.

#### Instructions/Recommendations:

1. **Choosing the Format:**

   - Decide whether you want your report to be diary-based (weekly evolution) or activity-based (step-by-step).
   - Clearly indicate your chosen format at the beginning of your report.
2. **Sections to Include:**

   **Introduction:**

   - Briefly introduce the OSS project.

   **Initial Research and Setup:**

   - Document the initial research you conducted about the project.
   - Explain how you set up your development environment.
   - Highlight any difficulties and how you overcame them.

   **First Contribution:**

   - Describe your first contribution to the project.
   - Explain the task you chose and why.
   - Discuss any challenges and how you addressed them.
   - Mention the feedback you received from the community.

   **Subsequent Contributions:**

   - For each subsequent contribution, detail the tasks you completed.
   - Discuss the learning curve and how your skills improved.
   - Mention any interactions with the project maintainers and community.

   **Final Contribution:**

   - Describe the last contribution you made to the project.
   - Reflect on how this contribution differed from your first.
   - Discuss the overall impact of your contributions on the project.

   **Pros and Cons:**

   - List the pros and cons of contributing to the project.
   - Include personal reflections and specific examples.

   **Lessons Learned:**

   - Reflect on the key lessons you learned during the process.
   - Discuss how these lessons will impact your future contributions to OSS.

   **Overall Reflection:**

   - Summarize your overall experience.
   - Reflect on how each step of the process helped you or your group.
   - Provide suggestions for future contributors.
3. **Submission Guidelines:**

   - Your report should be 1500-2500 words.
   - Use clear headings and subheadings for each section.
   - Include any relevant screenshots or code snippets as appendices.
   - Submit your report as a PDF file.
4. **Evaluation Criteria:**

   - **Completeness:** All sections are included and thoroughly discussed.
   - **Clarity:** The report is well-organized and easy to follow.
   - **Depth of Reflection:** The report includes detailed reflections on pros, cons, and lessons learned.
   - **Engagement:** Demonstrates active engagement with the OSS project and community.
   - **Quality of Writing:** The report is free of grammatical errors and well-written.

#### Tips:

- Write on-the-fly to capture real-time experiences and avoid forgetting details.
- Be honest about challenges and how you addressed them.
- Use this assignment as an opportunity to reflect on your growth and contributions.

#### Example Structure:

**Introduction**

- Project overview and selection reasons.

**Diary-Based Report:**

- Week 1:Initial research and Documentation Analysis.
- Week 2: Setting up the development environment.
- Week 3: First contribution and feedback.
- Week 4: Subsequent contributions and community interactions.
- Week 5: Final contribution and overall reflection.
- Pros and Cons.
- Lessons Learned.
- Overall Reflection.

**Activity-Based Report:**

- Initial Research and Setup.
- Documentation Analysis.
- First Contribution.
- Subsequent Contributions.
- Final Contribution.
- Pros and Cons.
- Lessons Learned.
- Overall Reflection.

By completing this assignment, you will gain valuable insights into the contribution process and be better prepared for future involvement in OSS projects.
