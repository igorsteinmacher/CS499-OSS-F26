# Lecture 2. The Cathedral and The Bazaar

> Canvas status: **active**

## **Summary**

In this lecture, you will be introduced to "The Cathedral and the Bazaar," an influential essay by Eric Raymond that explores the contrasting software development models of the traditional, hierarchical "cathedral" and the open, decentralized "bazaar."

Published in 1997, this work draws from Raymond's observations of the open-source movement, particularly his experience with the development of the Linux operating system and fetchmail. By comparing the two models, Raymond highlights the strengths and weaknesses of each approach, ultimately advocating for the collaborative and transparent nature of the bazaar.

His essay, not only mapped what Open Source was, and is, but also informed several current software development practices.
