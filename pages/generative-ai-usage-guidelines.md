### Ethics and AI

At NAU, the use of generative AI tools (such as ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, and others) is allowed when used responsibly and ethically. These tools can enhance learning, improve accessibility, and help students engage more deeply with their coursework. We will discuss how to engage with generative AI responsibly, aligning your use with NAU’s academic integrity standards and your own values.

#### "Offloading" Learning to AI

Offloading to AI means relying on it to remember information or make decisions for us. While AI can be a powerful tool, using it in place of genuine learning and critical thinking can limit your growth. The time and effort you invest in learning and practicing skills on your own will be far more rewarding—and will actually make you better at using AI thoughtfully and effectively later on.

#### AI as a Critical Thinking Teaching Tool

Some instructors may use generative AI (GenAI) in assignments to help you strengthen your critical thinking skills—and those experiences can be especially valuable. Take time to reflect on what you learn from those activities: what the AI reveals (or leaves out) about the course content, how its reasoning compares to your own, and how you can apply that process and insight to future learning.

### Course AI Policy

## ICON-yellow-ai@4x.pngLimited use of generative AI is permitted in specific contexts

This course allows for the use of generative artificial intelligence (AI) technologies as part of the research and preparation of the work.

The project deliverables **must be authored by the students** in the context of their own project. The documents must align with the actual product being developed and the (human!) experience students are having with the development. Therefore, you must not ask ChatGPT-like tools to write your documents or chunks of them, as it will include knowledge from their language training models. **Submitting final work authored by generative AI constitutes an academic integrity violation.**

However, for example, once you have your draft figured out, you can use generative AI to support your writing process, e.g., improving clarity and fixing grammar errors. You do not need to cite or otherwise acknowledge use of generative AI to support your writing when you use it as you might use other common tools and resources, like grammar-checking software or feedback from a reader. Ensure that your thinking and voice are dominant ,and be aware that it is the team's responsibility to critically evaluate any outcome obtained from AI support and correct or adapt to the team's reality.

For any additional use of generative AI, for example, if you need to include lines or passages of AI-generated text in a document, place them in quotation marks and cite the AI tool you used (generated with TOOLNAME). Any use of AI-generated content must be referenced in the document as such. Be careful! You'll have to implement this project; don't let ChatGPT decide what you have to turn in. This may lead to catastrophic consequences for your team!

Source code: Students are expected to write their own code for the project. However, if you need to include AI-generated code in your project, acknowledge the source in the source code as a comment. At the beginning of the generated code, add a comment stating "Generated with..." or "Adapted from..." and cite the AI tool you used. Add another comment at the end of the code to state that the generated code ended.

#### Academic Integrity Policy

AI tools are not treated as special cases; they fall under the same Academic Integrity Policy that governs all academic work at NAU.

That means:

- Submitting work generated by AI without proper attribution or instructor approval is considered academic misconduct, similar to plagiarism or cheating.
- Using AI for exams, assignments, or projects without permission violates academic integrity expectations.
- You are still responsible for the accuracy, originality, and ethical use of all submitted work, even if AI tools were used to help create it.

Read the [Academic Integrity Policy](https://www9.nau.edu/policies/Client/Details/307) in full to learn the scope of the policy before you make decisions about your conduct while completing and submitting coursework.

#### Student AI Use

The world of AI is in flux, and your ability to be dynamic and informed is necessary.  As a student, you will encounter a variety of course policies, use cases for and against AI use, and you will have to be diligent about how you conduct your coursework to align accordingly.

- Be aware of all AI use policies for all your courses.
- Use AI as an enhancement ot learning, not a replacement.
- Be ethical in your use of AI, including adherence to course policies, attribute AI outputs to yourself (cite AI use), and protect sensitive data and information by keeping it out of a GenAI tool.
- Have concerns about AI use? Discuss them with your instructors to identify possible alternatives and available assistance.

Please review the Office of the Provost's [webpage on AI Resources for Students](https://legacy.nau.edu/provost/academic-operations/resources-policies/artificial-intelligence/students/).

### Ethical Use of Data and GenAI

At Northern Arizona University, specific guidelines have been established regarding data use with AI. For instance**, even approved tools such as Microsoft Copilot must never be used for handling or processing materials classified as Level 3 or Level 4 under the University's Data Classification and Handling policy**. This restriction helps protect sensitive university data from unintended exposure.

[Review NAU's Data Classification and Handling Policy](https://in.nau.edu/its/data-classification/)

![Data Classification at NAU Level 4 is highly sensitive, Level 3 is Sensitive, Level 2 is internal, and Level 1 is public info.](https://canvas.nau.edu/courses/33255/files/16419879/preview)

#### Personal GenAI Accounts and Data Use

Users of GenAI with personal AND institution-supported accounts must be aware of which account is in use at any given time.  For example, many of us may have an NAU-supported Microsoft Copilot account and a personal Copilot account.  Users with a personal account should never put Level 2 data into a personal GenAI tool.
