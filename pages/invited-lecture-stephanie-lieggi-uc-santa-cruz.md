# Invited Lecture: Stephanie Lieggi (UC, Santa Cruz)

> Canvas status: **active**

**Title: What are Open Source Program Offices and Why They Matter**

**Bio**: Stephanie Lieggi is the Executive Director of the Center for Research in Open Source Software (CROSS) at the University of California, Santa Cruz. She supports academic-based open source projects and aims to create a sustainable contributor base through the establishment of hands-on mentorship programs. Since 2022, her role has also helped lead the UCSC Open Source Program Office (OSPO), supported by a grant from the Alfred P Sloan Foundation. Stephanie leads the effort to build a system-wide network of OSPOs at the University of California, securing financial support for building the network from the Sloan Foundation in Spring 2024.

**Abstract**: Open Source Program Offices (OSPOs) are dedicated organizational units that serve as centers of competency for open source strategy, compliance, and community engagement — originating in industry but now expanding rapidly into academic and government sectors. This presentation will go into more detail about how OSPOs developed in recent years and currently work across sectors.
